1. What is the primary advantage of using LoRA in federated fine-tuning of LLMs?

A. It increases model size for better accuracy
B. It allows full model training on each client
C. It reduces communication and memory by training only a small subset of parameters
D. It eliminates the need for aggregation
Answer: C

2. What was the main impact of non-IID data compared to IID data in the experiments?

A. It reduced communication cost significantly
B. It improved fairness across clients
C. It increased the client validation loss gap significantly
D. It eliminated overfitting
Answer: C

3. What was the approximate communication reduction achieved by using LoRA instead of full model transmission?

A. 10×
B. 50×
C. 114×
D. 500×
Answer: C

4. What was the effect of increasing client participation from 50% to 75%?

A. Increased fairness gap and reduced accuracy
B. Reduced fairness gap and improved validation performance
C. No noticeable change
D. Increased communication cost but reduced convergence
Answer: B

5. Why does validation loss show oscillatory behavior across rounds in federated learning?

A. Due to model initialization randomness
B. Because of centralized training instability
C. Due to client drift caused by heterogeneous local data distributions
D. Because the dataset is too small

Answer: C
